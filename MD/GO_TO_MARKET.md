# Go To Market

## Strategy Summary
Supply first. Hyper-local. Atlanta only. Launch July 2025. Prove the loop works before expanding anywhere.

Supply means both operator types from day one: mobile detailers who travel to the customer, and independent shop operators with a fixed location. The founder ran both — a mobile van and three physical shops in Atlanta simultaneously — and still handles logistics for those shops today. That direct operator network is the warmest recruitment channel in the building.

Discoverability is a first-class acquisition channel alongside paid social — not an afterthought. SEO, AEO, and App Store Optimization start before a single customer downloads the app. Reference AEO_SEO_GEO.md for the full discoverability strategy.

## Phase 1 — Supply Activation (Pre-Launch: Now → Launch)

### Goal
Get 15-25 active operators onboarded in Atlanta before a single customer downloads the app — a mix of mobile detailers and shop operators across the city.

### Why Supply First
A customer who opens the app and finds three operators with sparse profiles leaves and never comes back. A customer who finds 20 vetted, active, well-reviewed detailers and shops — both formats, multiple neighborhoods — becomes a regular. Density across both service models is the product on day one.

### Operator Recruitment Channels

**Personal Network (Highest Priority)**
- Former Foam Auto Spa contacts — operators, shop owners, suppliers, industry connections from both the mobile operation and the three shop locations
- The founder's ongoing logistics involvement with those shops creates warm, current relationships — not cold outreach to strangers
- Atlanta-area detailers and shop owners already known or connected to on social
- Warm outreach converts significantly higher than cold — start here and exhaust it fully before moving to cold

**Cold Outreach**
- Identify active mobile detailers via Instagram, Google, and Yelp
- Identify independent shop operators via Google Maps, Yelp, and local directory searches for "auto detailing Atlanta"
- Both channels require separate outreach angles — see Operator Type Acquisition Strategy below
- DM-based outreach for mobile operators. Phone/email for shop operators — they answer calls.
- Target: 100 outreach contacts to close 20-25 active operators across both types

**Social Media (Operator-Focused Content)**
- Instagram and TikTok content aimed at operators — not customers yet
- Content angles: Business tips for mobile detailers, bay management and walk-in strategy for shop owners, how to price services, how to retain customers, how to fill dead time
- Alternate between mobile-focused and shop-focused content weekly so both audiences feel seen
- This content builds credibility and generates warm inbound from operators who find it on their own

### Onboarding Incentive
Early operators — first 25, across both types — get:
- Extended free trial on Pro tier (3 months)
- "Founding Operator" badge on their profile permanently
- Direct feedback channel to the product team — their input shapes V2
- Zero platform booking fee for first 60 days

## Phase 2 — Soft Launch (Invite Only)

### Goal
Prove the full transaction loop works across both service models. Mobile customer books, operator shows up, payment processes, review gets submitted. Shop customer books a drop-off, operator completes the job, payment processes, review gets submitted.

### Launch Market
Atlanta, GA. Specific neighborhoods first — Buckhead, Midtown, Decatur, Sandy Springs, Smyrna. Areas with high density of car-conscious consumers and established operator supply across both mobile and fixed location types.

### Customer Acquisition (Invite Only Phase)
- Both mobile operators and shop operators invite their existing customers to book through the app
- This is the most powerful cold-start mechanic — existing trust, familiar relationships, just a new channel
- Mobile operators are motivated because it simplifies their booking and payment workflow
- Shop operators are motivated because it gives them an online booking presence many of them don't currently have

### Success Metrics for Soft Launch
- 15+ active operators completing jobs through the app (mix of both types)
- 100+ bookings processed across both service models
- 4.5+ average rating
- Payment flow working end-to-end with zero issues for both mobile and drop-off flows
- Operators using the app to manage their day, not just as a booking portal

## Phase 3 — Public Launch

### Goal
Open the app to all Atlanta customers. Begin building demand-side awareness across all acquisition channels simultaneously — for both mobile booking and shop/drop-off booking.

### Customer Acquisition Channels

**Organic Search / SEO (Long-Term Primary Channel)**
- foamauto.com is the discoverability engine — for all operator types and customers
- Operator profile pages indexed by Google from day one — every operator onboarded is an SEO asset, mobile or shop
- City hub pages rank for "[city] mobile detailing," "[city] detailing shops near me," and "book detailer [neighborhood]"
- Blog content publishing at 2 posts/week minimum from launch, scaling to 4/week by Month 9
- Targets both operator types in search and both customer intents (come to me vs. drop off nearby)
- Full strategy: reference AEO_SEO_GEO.md

**AEO — AI Answer Engine Presence**
- foamauto.com content structured to be cited by ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, and Siri
- FAQ pages on all major product pages with FAQPage schema markup
- Question-first content structure so AI assistants can directly pull and cite FOAM answers
- Target: appearing in AI answers for "best mobile detailing app," "auto detailing shop software," "how to find a mobile detailer," and "[city] detailing" queries within 90 days of launch
- Full strategy: reference AEO_SEO_GEO.md

**App Store Optimization (ASO)**
- iOS App Store and Google Play listings optimized for keyword ranking before public launch
- Targets: "mobile detailing app," "car detailing booking," "detailing business app," "detailing shop software" in app store search
- Screenshot strategy treated as conversion creative — show both mobile and shop operator experiences
- Review collection built into all operator and customer post-job flows

**Paid Social (Launch Accelerant)**
- Instagram and TikTok ads targeting Atlanta metro
- Audience: 25-45, vehicle owners, disposable income, convenience-oriented
- Three creative tracks: mobile detailer acquisition, shop operator acquisition, customer acquisition
- Mobile operator creative: "Still managing your bookings through Instagram?"
- Shop operator creative: "Still running your shop on a whiteboard?"
- Customer creative: before/after visuals, "book a mobile detailer or find a shop near you in 2 minutes"
- Rain Coverage membership as a hook ad across both customer intents

**Organic Social**
- Document the build and launch publicly on Instagram/TikTok
- Behind-the-scenes of onboarding operators — show both the mobile detailer getting their profile set up and the shop owner configuring their bays
- Founder-led content — the story of running a van and three shops simultaneously is worth telling, more than once, across multiple formats
- First-gen American building in Atlanta is a compelling story — own it

**Referral Loop**
- Customer refers a friend, both get credit toward their next booking
- Mobile operators share their booking link on their own social — their followers become FOAM customers
- Shop operators get a booking link to display in-location — physical QR code at the bay, on the counter, in their Google Business Profile

**Local Press and Community**
- Atlanta Business Chronicle, Axios Atlanta, local lifestyle blogs
- Black Enterprise, Essence — the founder story fits and these audiences over-index on brand loyalty
- "Atlanta startup building the operating system for an $18.7B detailing industry — for both the mobile operators and the mom-and-pop shops" is the right story, and it's stronger than mobile-only
- Press placements serve double duty: customer awareness and AEO entity building
- Target at least one placed story at or before public launch

**Partnerships (Explore Post-Launch)**
- Car dealerships — new car buyers are primed detailing customers for both mobile and shop services
- Apartment complexes and HOAs — captive audience, bulk booking potential for mobile operators
- Corporate fleet managers — longer-term B2B play for both mobile and shop dispatch

## Operator Type Acquisition Strategy

FOAM serves two operator types. Acquisition messaging, channels, and outreach style differ by type.

### Mobile Operators
- Primary pain: running business through DMs and Venmo, losing leads, no route management, no crew tools
- Primary message: "Stop running your business like a side hustle"
- Primary channels: Instagram/TikTok organic, blog content, cold DM outreach to active mobile detailers on social
- Search keywords: "mobile detailing app," "app for mobile detailers," "detailing business software"
- Outreach style: Social-first. Mobile operators live on Instagram. Meet them there.

### Fixed Location / Shop Operators
- Primary pain: dead bays, walk-in unpredictability, no scheduling system built for detailing, no customer retention tools
- Primary message: "Fill your bays. Keep your customers coming back."
- Primary channels: Google search (shop operators search more than they scroll social), foamauto.com/for-shops content, phone and email outreach, industry directory listings
- Search keywords: "auto detailing shop software," "detailing shop booking system," "detailing studio management app"
- Outreach style: Phone and email first. Shop owners are not always on Instagram. A direct call with a specific pitch — "we built bay management and drop-off scheduling specifically for shops" — converts better than a DM.
- Strategic note: this keyword universe has significantly less competition than the mobile space — first-mover content advantage is real and should be captured early

## National Expansion Acquisition Model

When FOAM opens Market 2, the acquisition playbook repeats for both operator types:

**60 days before launch:** Publish city hub page on foamauto.com with "coming soon" messaging. Begin ranking for "[city] mobile detailing app" and "[city] detailing shops" before a single operator is onboarded.

**45 days before launch:** Content push targeting both operator types in that city. Same blog content pillar applied to the new market geography with local keyword specificity.

**30 days before launch:** Local citation building — Google Business Profile, Yelp, Apple Maps, local Chamber directory. NAP consistency established.

**Launch day:** Activate booking on city page. Operator profile pages go live and begin indexing.

**30-90 days post-launch:** Build neighborhood pages as operator density grows. Pursue local press. Referral loop activated in new market.

Market 2 priority candidates: Charlotte NC, Nashville TN, Miami FL, Dallas TX, Houston TX.

## What Not to Do at Launch
- Do not launch in multiple cities simultaneously
- Do not run customer acquisition ads before operator supply — both mobile and shop — is ready
- Do not offer discounts so deep they attract the wrong customer or undervalue operators
- Do not add V2 features before V1 is working perfectly
- Do not pitch investors until the soft launch loop is proven across both service models
- Do not treat SEO as a later-stage task — the domain needs index history from day one
- Do not use stock photography in marketing — authenticity is core to the brand
- Do not publish blog content without proper on-page SEO structure
- Do not run only mobile-focused marketing — shop operators need to see themselves in the brand from day one
