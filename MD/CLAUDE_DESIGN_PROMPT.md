## PROMPT

Design a 12-slide pitch deck for FOAM, an auto detailing startup pitching Y Combinator. The deck should feel like the best YC decks you've seen — sharp, minimal, one idea per slide, built for a partner who's seen 800 decks this week. Not a corporate PowerPoint. A confident founder's deck.

---

### Brand
- **Name:** FOAM
- **Tagline:** Your business. Your car. Clean.
- **Primary color:** Cobalt blue (#2563EB) as accent on dark backgrounds
- **Background:** Near-black (#0D0D0D) for hero slides, very dark gray (#161616) for content slides
- **Typography:** Outfit (display, headlines) + Inter (body, data)
- **Tone:** Confident. Operator-credible. Not startup-generic. This brand has grease under its fingernails.

---

### Deck Structure — 12 Slides

**Slide 1 — Cover**
- FOAM wordmark centered
- One line: "The operating system for auto detailing."
- Sub-line: "Mobile detailers. Independent shops. Atlanta, GA. Launching 2026."
- Dark background, cobalt accent

**Slide 2 — The Problem**
- Headline: "An $18.7B industry runs on Instagram DMs, Venmo, and whiteboards."
- Three bullets:
  - 192,000+ US auto detailers manage their business across 5+ disconnected apps
  - Independent shop owners track their bays on whiteboards and their customers in a notes app
  - Customers can't find, vet, or trust who's handling their car — mobile or shop
- Visual: split image concept — chaotic Instagram DM thread on the left, a bay whiteboard on the right

**Slide 3 — Why This Hasn't Been Solved**
- Headline: "Every booking app treats the operator like a lead. We treat them like the product."
- The Cut did this for barbers. Toast did it for restaurants. Nobody has done it for detailing — mobile or fixed.
- Spiffy abandoned independent operators to chase B2B fleet. Generic tools like Booksy were never built for a van or a bay.
- Visual: 2x2 matrix — "Built for operators" vs "Marketplace for customers" — FOAM alone in the upper right

**Slide 4 — The Solution**
- Headline: "One app. Two service models. Three operator types."
- Three columns: Mobile Operator (travels to customer), Shop Operator (customer drops off), Hybrid (runs both)
- Customer discovers both in one feed
- Visual: three phone mockups side by side — mobile operator, shop operator, and customer app views

**Slide 5 — Why Now**
- Headline: "The shift to app-based booking is happening now. Mobile detailing is growing 19% a year."
- Mobile/on-demand sub-segment: 19.35% CAGR through 2030
- App-based bookings across the category: 24.96% CAGR through 2030
- Overall industry: flat. The growth is in the channel shift.
- Visual: two lines diverging — walk-in declining, app-booked accelerating

**Slide 6 — Market Size**
- Headline: "$18.7B today. $22B by 2035. Atlanta alone is $110M–$230M annually."
- US car wash + detailing: $18.7B (IBISWorld 2026)
- 57,900+ detailing-related US businesses — mobile and fixed
- Atlanta bottom-up: $80M–$170M consumer + $30M–$60M B2B
- Visual: nested TAM/SAM/SOM boxes — Atlanta first

**Slide 7 — The Founder**
- Headline: "I ran both sides of this business. The tools didn't exist for either."
- Four lines:
  - Ran Foam Auto Spa in Atlanta — a mobile van and three physical shops simultaneously
  - Chased Venmo on the mobile side. Tracked bays on a whiteboard on the shop side. Both were a disaster.
  - Still handles logistics for those shops today. This problem is current, not historical.
  - FOAM is the OS I needed when I was loading the van at 6am and opening the bay at 7am.
- Visual: founder photo — if available, with both a mobile setup and a shop environment

**Slide 8 — Business Model**
- Headline: "Subscription + booking fees + B2B retainers. Live at launch."
- Tiers: Starter $34/mo · Pro $79/mo · Crew $174/mo (same for both operator types)
- Per-booking fees: $5–$10 per job
- B2B fleet retainers: $500–$5,000+/month
- Unit economics: Pro mobile detailer = $279/mo = $3,348 ARR. Shop operator on Crew tier = higher.
- Path: 100 operators = $300K ARR. 500 operators = $1.8M ARR.
- Visual: clean revenue bar chart showing ramp across 100, 250, 500 operators

**Slide 9 — The Moat**
- Headline: "90 days in, they can't leave. Their entire business lives here."
- Mobile operators lock in: route history, customer geography, crew tracking
- Shop operators lock in: bay utilization, walk-in conversion, service performance, customer return rate
- Different data. Same switching cost.
- Uncopyable: founded by someone who ran both formats. That operator fluency is not acquirable.
- Visual: a single powerful quote from a founding operator (mobile or shop)

**Slide 10 — Traction**
- Headline: "Atlanta launches [Month]. [X] operators committed. Both formats represented."
- Specific numbers: founding mobile operators, founding shop operators, customers on waitlist
- App status: MVP in build, Stripe Connect integrated, App Store submission [date]
- foamauto.com: [N] organic sessions/month, [N] keywords ranking
- Visual: launch timeline — Foundation → Soft Launch → Public Launch → Market 2

**Slide 11 — The Vision**
- Headline: "Detailing is the first vertical. Fragmented service categories are the play."
- Phase 1: Own Atlanta detailing — mobile and shops
- Phase 2: National infrastructure layer for the full detailing industry
- Phase 3: The operator OS + marketplace model ports to every service category where the worker is skilled and the software is bad
- Visual: expanding map or category stack — detailing to adjacent verticals

**Slide 12 — The Ask**
- Headline: "[Ask Amount] to [Key Milestone] in [Timeline]."
- Three lines: what the capital does (hire, ship, hit GMV target, expand to Market 2)
- Close line: "Built by an operator who ran both sides of this industry. For every operator still running it today."
- Visual: clean typography, FOAM wordmark, no decoration

---

### Design Direction
- Minimal. One idea per slide. White space is intentional.
- Headlines should be the largest element on most slides — they carry the argument
- Data visualizations should be simple, readable, and built with the brand color
- Phone mockups on Slide 4 should be high-fidelity enough to show the operator experience difference between mobile and shop flows
- The founder slide (7) should feel human — not corporate. The most personal slide in the deck.
- Never use stock photography. Every image should feel earned, not placed.
- The deck should feel like it could be dropped in front of a YC partner and hold its own in under 3 minutes of scanning.
