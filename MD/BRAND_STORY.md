# FOAM — Brand Story

---

## The Origin Story
*Hero's Journey · 200 words · For the about page, pitch deck, press kit, and founder's voice*

---

Every detailer I've ever met is brilliant at the work and exhausted by everything around it.

I know because I was one of them. I ran Foam Auto Spa — a mobile detailing operation I built with my own hands. I knew how to make a car look like it just left the showroom. What I didn't have was a single tool that helped me run the actual business. I chased payments through Venmo. I lost customer notes when my phone died. I managed my crew through a group text that nobody read on time. I was good at the craft and terrible at the infrastructure — not because I wasn't capable, but because the infrastructure didn't exist.

So I closed the business. And I couldn't stop thinking about every detailer still out there doing exactly what I did.

That's why FOAM exists.

Not because I studied the problem. Because I lived it. FOAM is the operating system I needed when I was loading my van at 6am — built for the detailers doing that right now, and for every customer who just wants to find someone they can trust.

The work deserves better tools. So do the people doing it.

### Medium Version — About Page / Investor One-Pager
*~350 words*

There's a version of building a company where you read market research, identify a gap, and launch a product. That's not this.

FOAM started with a van, a pressure washer, and a business called Foam Auto Spa. Mobile detailing in Atlanta — showing up to driveways, parking lots, and office complexes to do work most people don't think about until their car embarrasses them at the wrong moment. The craft was real. The results were real. The business was real.

The infrastructure was a disaster.

Scheduling happened through Instagram DMs and text messages. Payments got chased through Venmo — sometimes collected, sometimes not. Customer vehicle notes lived in a phone one cracked screen away from disappearing permanently. Crew schedules got managed through a group chat that had too many messages and not enough answers. Every other industry had tools built for it. Detailing had workarounds dressed up as systems.

When the business closed, the problem didn't go away. It stayed. Because out there, right now, there are thousands of detailers — solo operators building something from nothing, crew bosses managing multiple vans and multiple schedules — running their entire business the exact same way. With duct tape and determination and the constant, low-grade stress of a professional who is excellent at their craft and completely unsupported as a business operator.

FOAM is the answer to that problem. Not from the outside looking in — from the inside, with grease under the fingernails and a very specific memory of what it felt like to not have what you needed.

The platform gives detailers the complete business infrastructure the industry was never given: booking, payments, customer history, crew management, route logistics, and business reporting — purpose-built for the specific reality of mobile work. And it gives customers something they've been missing too: a real way to find, book, and trust the best detailers in their area.

The work was always there. The tools finally are too.

### Long Version — Full Founder Narrative / Pitch Deck / Press Profile
*~600 words*

The detailing industry doesn't have a talent problem. It has an infrastructure problem.

The professionals doing this work are exceptional. They wake up before most people set their alarms. They load vans with hundreds of dollars of product. They spend hours in direct sun, on their hands and knees, transforming vehicles in ways that require genuine skill — paint correction, ceramic coatings, full interior restorations. This is real craft. The kind of work that takes years to master and matters deeply to the people who do it.

What they've never had is a business built around them.

I know this because I was one of them. Before FOAM, I ran Foam Auto Spa — a mobile detailing operation in Atlanta. The name, the van, the equipment, the client list — all mine. I was good at the work. What I wasn't good at was managing everything that surrounded it, and that wasn't my fault. The tools I needed simply didn't exist.

Booking came through Instagram DMs and phone calls. A customer would message at 11pm, I'd see it at 7am, we'd trade messages for twenty minutes agreeing on a time, and I'd write it in my phone calendar hoping I didn't double-book. Payment collection meant sending Venmo requests and watching the "pending" notification sit there for three days while I wondered whether to follow up and seem desperate or stay quiet and seem unbothered. Neither felt right.

Customer vehicle history — the notes about that BMW whose clear coat needed careful handling, or the truck owner who wanted extra attention to the running boards — lived in a notes app that was one phone upgrade away from being gone forever. Crew scheduling happened through a group text that somehow everyone was always too busy to read at the moment it mattered. Every time something fell through the cracks, it wasn't because I was bad at business. It was because no one had ever built the infrastructure this business needed.

I closed Foam Auto Spa. The economics of mobile detailing without the right tools eventually catch up to you. But the problem I'd lived inside for years didn't close with it. It followed me — because I knew, with absolute certainty, that every detailer out there was running the exact same playbook. Not because they wanted to. Because there was no other option.

That certainty became FOAM.

The platform exists because the industry deserves what every other industry already has. Restaurants have Toast. Salons have Vagaro. Barbershops have The Cut. Mobile detailing had a group chat and a Venmo balance. FOAM closes that gap — with booking, payments, crew management, customer history, route logistics, and business reporting all built specifically for the way mobile detailing actually works. Travel time between jobs. Vehicle-specific service notes. Crew accountability in the field. Recurring customer relationships that turn a side hustle into a real business.

And on the customer side — the person with a dirty car and no idea how to find someone trustworthy to clean it — FOAM gives them something the industry has never reliably provided: a way to find the best detailers in their area, see real results from real jobs, and book in under two minutes.

This isn't a platform built by someone who read a report about the mobile services market. It's built by someone who ran the business, felt the frustration, and refused to accept that the detailing industry had to keep operating like it was 2009.

The work was always worth building better tools for. The people doing it were always worth building for.

FOAM is the proof.
