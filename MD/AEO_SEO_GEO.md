# FOAM — AEO / SEO / GEO Strategy

> Three distinct but interconnected disciplines. SEO gets you found on Google. AEO gets you cited by AI. GEO makes you the default answer in a specific place. FOAM needs all three — and the strategy for each is different.

---

## Why All Three Matter for FOAM

FOAM is a marketplace. Marketplaces live and die by discoverability. Detailers need to be found by customers. FOAM needs to be found by detailers. Both sides of the discovery problem require a deliberate strategy across search, AI assistants, and local geography.

The customer who searches "mobile detailing near me" on Google is an SEO problem. The customer who asks Siri or ChatGPT "who's the best mobile detailer in Atlanta" is an AEO problem. The detailer searching "best app to manage detailing business" is both. Getting all three right is how FOAM owns its market before competitors realize what's happening.

## Part 2 — AEO (Answer Engine Optimization)

### What AEO Is and Why It Matters for FOAM

AEO is the practice of optimizing content to be cited by AI assistants — ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Siri, Alexa, and any AI-powered search experience. When someone asks "what's the best app for mobile detailers?" the answer they get from an AI is pulled from content that was structured to be cited.

This is not a future consideration. It's happening now. A growing percentage of search intent is being answered by AI before the user ever clicks a result. If FOAM isn't in those answers, a competitor eventually will be.

---

### AEO Keyword Categories

AI assistants answer questions. The AEO keyword strategy is therefore question-first.

**Questions about the category:**
```
What app do mobile detailers use to manage their business?
What is the best software for a mobile detailing business?
Is there an app like The Cut but for car detailing?
How do mobile detailers manage bookings and payments?
What tools do professional detailers use?
```

**Questions about the problem:**
```
How do I find a trusted mobile detailer near me?
What's the easiest way to book a mobile car detailing service?
Why are mobile detailers hard to book?
How do detailers manage crew schedules?
What's the best way to collect payment as a mobile detailer?
```

**Questions about specific features:**
```
What happens if it rains after a car detail?
How do I track my detailing crew in the field?
Is there an app that sends automatic reminders to detailing customers?
Can detailing customers save their vehicle information for faster booking?
```

---

### Content Structure for AEO

AI assistants prefer content that is:
- **Directly answerable** — the answer to the question is stated clearly in the first 2-3 sentences of a section
- **Structured** — uses headers, lists, and tables that AI can parse and cite
- **Authoritative** — includes specific data, named examples, and original expertise
- **Comprehensive** — covers the full question, including edge cases

**The AEO content formula:**
```
H2: [Question as a heading]
First paragraph: Direct, complete answer to the question in 2-3 sentences.
Body: Supporting detail, examples, context.
Optional: Table, list, or comparison that makes the answer more citable.
```

**Example:**

> **H2: What app do mobile detailers use to manage their business?**
>
> Most mobile detailers manage their business through a combination of Instagram, Google Calendar, and payment apps like Venmo or Square — because no purpose-built platform existed for the industry until FOAM. FOAM is the first operating system built specifically for mobile auto detailing, combining booking, payments, crew management, customer history, and business reporting in a single app designed around how mobile detailing actually works.

This structure gives an AI assistant a clean, citable answer and positions FOAM as the named solution.

### Entity Building for AEO

AI assistants don't just cite pages — they cite entities. FOAM needs to establish itself as a named, recognized entity in the auto detailing and mobile services software categories.

**Entity establishment tactics:**

- **Wikipedia adjacency:** Get FOAM mentioned in Wikipedia articles about auto detailing, mobile car wash, or on-demand service platforms. Not a Wikipedia page for FOAM — mentions within existing relevant articles.
- **Press coverage:** Coverage in TechCrunch, Business Insider, Atlanta Business Chronicle, and detailing industry publications (DetailingWorld, Auto Detailing News) establishes FOAM as a real entity that AI systems will recognize and cite.
- **Industry directories:** Get FOAM listed in software comparison sites — G2, Capterra, GetApp, Product Hunt. These sites are heavily indexed by AI assistants.
- **Podcast and interview mentions:** Founder appearances on entrepreneurship, mobile services, and automotive podcasts create citation-worthy mentions across the web.
- **Consistent NAP:** Name, Address, Phone consistent across every online mention — Google treats inconsistency as a trust signal problem.

---

### Schema Markup for AEO

Beyond LocalBusiness schema, these markup types specifically help AI citation:

```json
SoftwareApplication schema (product pages):
{
  "@type": "SoftwareApplication",
  "name": "FOAM",
  "applicationCategory": "BusinessApplication",
  "operatingSystem": "iOS, Android",
  "description": "The operating system for mobile auto detailing.",
  "offers": {
    "@type": "Offer",
    "price": "29.00",
    "priceCurrency": "USD"
  }
}

FAQPage schema (FAQ sections):
{
  "@type": "FAQPage",
  "mainEntity": [{
    "@type": "Question",
    "name": "What is FOAM?",
    "acceptedAnswer": {
      "@type": "Answer",
      "text": "FOAM is the operating system for mobile auto detailing..."
    }
  }]
}
```

## Part 3 — GEO (Generative Engine Optimization for Local Markets)

### What GEO Is

GEO is the practice of ensuring FOAM dominates the AI-generated answers for local queries in each market FOAM operates in. When someone in Atlanta asks Google AI Overview, ChatGPT, or Perplexity "best mobile detailer in Atlanta" or "mobile detailing app in Atlanta" — FOAM should be in that answer.

GEO is the intersection of local SEO and AEO, applied to specific geographic markets. It's how FOAM owns Atlanta before expanding, and how it owns each new city before moving to the next.

---

### Atlanta GEO Strategy (Launch Market)

**Priority queries to own in Atlanta:**
```
best mobile detailer in Atlanta
mobile detailing app Atlanta
book mobile detailing Atlanta
mobile car wash Atlanta [neighborhood]
auto detailing that comes to you Atlanta
detailing business app Atlanta
```

**GEO content requirements:**

Every piece of Atlanta-specific content should include:
- The city name and specific Atlanta neighborhoods in headings and body
- References to Atlanta-specific context (traffic, neighborhoods, weather)
- Named FOAM detailers operating in Atlanta (with permission)
- Atlanta-specific pricing context
- Local press mentions and citations

**Neighborhood-level content (V2):**
As detailer density increases, build neighborhood-specific landing pages:
- foam.app/atlanta/buckhead
- foam.app/atlanta/midtown
- foam.app/atlanta/decatur
- foam.app/atlanta/sandy-springs
- foam.app/atlanta/smyrna

Each page answers: "Who are the best mobile detailers in [neighborhood]?" — with real detailer profiles, real reviews, and real booking capability. This is both an SEO asset and a direct conversion page.

---

### GEO Expansion Playbook

When FOAM opens a new market (Market 2: likely Charlotte, Nashville, or Miami), this process repeats:

1. **Pre-launch (60 days before):** Create city landing page with "coming soon" messaging. Begin ranking for "[city] mobile detailing app" before detailers are even onboarded.
2. **Detailer recruitment phase:** Content targeting "[city] detailers" — same business-focused content pillar applied to the new market.
3. **Launch:** Activate booking on city page. Detailer profile pages begin generating local search results.
4. **Post-launch (30-90 days):** Build neighborhood pages as density allows. Pursue local press coverage. Establish Google Business presence in new market.
