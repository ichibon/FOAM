# FOAM — Brand Story

---

## The Origin Story
*Hero's Journey · 200 words · For the about page, pitch deck, press kit, and founder's voice*

---

Every detailer I've ever met is brilliant at the work and exhausted by everything around it.

I know because I was one of them — both kinds. Foam Auto Spa was a mobile van hitting driveways across Atlanta and three physical shops running bays at the same time. I knew how to make a car look like it just left the showroom. What I didn't have was a single tool that helped me run any of it. I chased payments through Venmo. I lost customer notes when my phone died. I managed mobile crew through a group text nobody read and shop walk-ins on a whiteboard I updated by hand. I was good at the craft. I was terrible at the infrastructure — not because I wasn't capable, but because the infrastructure didn't exist for either model.

I still handle logistics for those shops today. The problem hasn't gone away. It just convinced me that FOAM needed to exist.

Not because I studied this industry. Because I ran it. Both sides of it. FOAM is the operating system I needed when I was loading the van at 6am and opening the bay at 7am — built for every operator still doing exactly that.

The work deserves better tools. So do the people doing it.

### Medium Version — About Page / Investor One-Pager
*~350 words*

There's a version of building a company where you read market research, identify a gap, and launch a product. That's not this.

FOAM started with a van, a pressure washer, three shop locations, and a business called Foam Auto Spa. Mobile detailing showing up to driveways and parking lots. Fixed location bays processing walk-ins and drop-offs. Both running simultaneously in Atlanta. The craft was real on both sides. The results were real. The business was real.

The infrastructure was a disaster on both sides.

Mobile bookings came through Instagram DMs and text messages. Payments got chased through Venmo. Customer vehicle notes lived in a phone one cracked screen away from disappearing permanently. Crew schedules for the mobile side got managed through a group chat that had too many messages and not enough answers. The shop side wasn't any cleaner — bay assignments tracked on a whiteboard, walk-in volume unpredictable, no way to see which customers came back and which never returned. Every other industry had tools built for it. Detailing had workarounds dressed up as systems, and it didn't matter whether you ran a van or a bay.

The founder still handles logistics for those shops today. The problem didn't close when Foam Auto Spa did. It followed him — because out there, right now, there are thousands of mobile detailers and thousands of independent shop owners running their entire businesses the exact same way. With duct tape and determination and the constant low-grade stress of a professional who is excellent at the craft and completely unsupported as a business operator.

FOAM is the answer to that problem. Not from the outside looking in — from the inside, with grease under the fingernails and a very specific memory of what it felt like to manage a route and a bay at the same time and have no software built for either.

The platform gives every operator the business infrastructure this industry was never given. And it gives customers something they've been missing too: a real way to find, book, and trust the best detailers and shops in their area.

The work was always there. The tools finally are too.

### Long Version — Full Founder Narrative / Pitch Deck / Press Profile
*~600 words*

The detailing industry doesn't have a talent problem. It has an infrastructure problem. And it has that problem on both sides of the operation.

The professionals doing this work are exceptional. Mobile detailers wake up before most people set their alarms, load vans with hundreds of dollars of product, and spend hours in direct sun transforming vehicles with genuine skill. Shop owners open their bays early, manage walk-ins and booked appointments simultaneously, and run operations that require real logistics — scheduling, staffing, customer communication, bay utilization — with none of the tools that other service industries take for granted. Paint correction, ceramic coatings, full interior restorations. This is real craft across both models. The kind of work that takes years to master and matters deeply to the people who do it.

What neither side has ever had is a business built around them.

I know this because I ran both. Before FOAM, I ran Foam Auto Spa — not just a mobile van, but three physical shop locations in Atlanta alongside it. I was managing a crew on the road and a crew in the bays at the same time. The problems were different in their specifics and identical in their structure: no software understood either one of us.

Mobile bookings came through Instagram DMs and phone calls. A customer would message at 11pm, I'd see it at 7am, and we'd spend twenty minutes agreeing on a time I'd write in my phone calendar hoping I didn't double-book against a route that was already stretching the day thin. On the shop side, walk-ins showed up without warning against a whiteboard that was always two updates behind reality. Payment collection meant chasing Venmo on the mobile side and processing cards through a system that had no idea who this customer was or what their car needed. Customer vehicle history — the notes about the BMW whose clear coat needed careful handling, or the truck owner who always wanted extra time on the running boards — lived in a notes app that was one phone upgrade away from being gone forever. Crew scheduling for both channels happened through group texts that somehow everyone was always too busy to read at the moment it mattered.

Every time something fell through the cracks, it wasn't because I was bad at business. It was because no one had ever built the infrastructure this business needed — on either side of it.

I still handle logistics for those shops today. Which means this isn't a story about someone who lived the problem and moved on. It's a story about someone who is still in it, still close enough to it to feel every product decision in his bones.

That proximity became FOAM.

The platform exists because the detailing industry deserves what every other service industry already has. Restaurants have Toast. Salons have Vagaro. Barbershops have The Cut. Mobile detailers had a group chat and a Venmo balance. Shop owners had a whiteboard and a generic POS system that had never heard of a detail job. FOAM closes both gaps simultaneously — with booking, payments, crew management, customer history, route logistics, bay management, and business reporting built specifically for the way detailing actually works across both service models.

And on the customer side — the person with a dirty car who doesn't know whether they want someone to come to them or whether they want to drop it off somewhere they trust — FOAM gives them something the industry has never reliably provided: a way to find the best operators in their area, see real results from real jobs, and book in under two minutes regardless of which model fits their day.

This isn't a platform built by someone who read a report about the mobile services market. It's built by someone who ran both sides of the business, felt every version of the frustration, and refused to accept that the detailing industry had to keep operating like it was 2009.

The work was always worth building better tools for. The people doing it — in every format — were always worth building for.

FOAM is the proof.
