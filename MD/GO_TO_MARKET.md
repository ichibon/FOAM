# Go To Market

## Strategy Summary
Supply first. Hyper-local. Atlanta only. Launch July 2025. Prove the loop works before expanding anywhere.

Discoverability is a first-class acquisition channel alongside paid social — not an afterthought. SEO, AEO, and App Store Optimization start before a single customer downloads the app. Reference AEO_SEO_GEO.md for the full discoverability strategy.

---

## Phase 1 — Supply Activation (Pre-Launch: Now → Launch)

### Goal
Get 15-25 active detailers onboarded in Atlanta before a single customer downloads the app.

### Why Supply First
A customer who opens the app and finds 3 detailers with sparse profiles leaves and never comes back. A customer who finds 20 vetted, active, well-reviewed detailers becomes a regular. Density is the product on day one.

### Detailer Recruitment Channels

**Personal Network (Highest Priority)**
- Former Foam Auto Spa contacts — operators, suppliers, industry connections
- Atlanta-area detailers already known or connected to on social
- Warm outreach converts significantly higher than cold — start here

**Cold Outreach**
- Identify active mobile detailers and shop operators in Atlanta via Instagram, Google, and Yelp
- DM-based outreach — direct, personal, not templated
- Key message: "We're building the operating system for detailers. You get the back office tools free during beta. We help you grow."
- Target: 100 outreach contacts to close 20-25 active operators across both mobile and fixed location types

**Social Media (Detailer-Focused Content)**
- Instagram and TikTok content aimed at operators — not customers yet
- Content angle: Business tips, how to price services, how to manage crew, how to retain customers, how to fill bays
- This builds credibility and warm inbound from operators who find the content
- Same content seeded on foamauto.com/blog begins building SEO authority before launch

### Onboarding Incentive
Early operators (first 25) get:
- Extended free trial on Pro tier (3 months)
- "Founding Operator" badge on their profile permanently
- Direct feedback channel to product team — their input shapes V2
- Zero platform booking fee for first 60 days

## Phase 2 — Soft Launch (Invite Only)

### Goal
Prove the full transaction loop works. Customer books. Operator shows up. Payment processes. Review gets submitted.

### Launch Market
Atlanta, GA. Specific neighborhoods first — Buckhead, Midtown, Decatur, Sandy Springs, Smyrna. Areas with high density of car-conscious consumers and established operator supply across both mobile and fixed location types.

### Customer Acquisition (Invite Only Phase)
- Operators invite their existing customers to book through the app
- This is the most powerful cold-start mechanic — existing trust, familiar relationships, just a new channel
- Operators are motivated because it simplifies their booking and payment workflow

### Success Metrics for Soft Launch
- 15+ active operators completing jobs through app
- 100+ bookings processed
- 4.5+ average rating
- Payment flow working end-to-end with zero issues
- Operators using the app to manage their day, not just as a booking portal

## Phase 3 — Public Launch

### Goal
Open the app to all Atlanta customers. Begin building demand-side awareness and habit across all acquisition channels simultaneously.

### Customer Acquisition Channels

**Organic Search / SEO (Long-Term Primary Channel)**
- foamauto.com is the discoverability engine — both for operators and customers
- Operator profile pages indexed by Google from day one — every operator onboarded is an SEO asset
- City hub pages rank for "[city] mobile detailing," "[city] detailing shops," and "book detailer [neighborhood]"
- Blog content publishing at 2 posts/week minimum from launch, scaling to 4/week by Month 9
- Targets both mobile operator searches ("mobile detailing app") and customer searches ("mobile detailing near me")
- This channel compounds over 6-18 months and eventually becomes the lowest-cost acquisition source
- Full strategy: reference AEO_SEO_GEO.md

**AEO — AI Answer Engine Presence**
- foamauto.com content structured to be cited by ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, and Siri
- FAQ pages on all major product pages with FAQPage schema markup
- Question-first content structure so AI assistants can directly pull and cite FOAM answers
- Target: appearing in AI answers for "best mobile detailing app," "how to find a mobile detailer," and "[city] mobile detailing" queries within 90 days of launch
- Full strategy: reference AEO_SEO_GEO.md

**App Store Optimization (ASO)**
- iOS App Store and Google Play listings optimized for keyword ranking before public launch
- Targets: "mobile detailing app," "car detailing booking," "detailing business app" in app store search
- Screenshot strategy treated as conversion creative — each screen communicates one clear value proposition
- Review collection built into operator and customer post-job flows
- ASO is AEO for app stores — when Siri or Google Assistant answers "best mobile detailing app," the App Store listing is part of what gets surfaced

**Paid Social (Launch Accelerant)**
- Instagram and TikTok ads targeting Atlanta metro
- Audience: 25-45, vehicle owners, disposable income, convenience-oriented
- Creative angle: Before/after videos, "your detailer comes to you" lifestyle content
- Shop operator angle: "Drop your car off, pick it up perfect"
- Rain Coverage membership as a hook ad — highly shareable, emotionally resonant
- Role: accelerates what organic is building, not a replacement for it

**Organic Social**
- Document the build and launch publicly on Instagram/TikTok
- Behind-the-scenes of onboarding operators, the founder story, Foam Auto Spa origin
- Founder-led content — your face and voice, not just a brand page
- First-gen American building in Atlanta is a compelling story — own it
- Social content and blog content feed each other — repurpose blog posts into social, use social engagement signals to inform blog topics

**Referral Loop**
- Customer refers a friend, both get credit toward their next booking
- Operator shares booking link on their own social — their followers become FOAM customers
- Every operator's existing audience is organic distribution

**Local Press and Community**
- Atlanta Business Chronicle, Axios Atlanta, local lifestyle blogs
- Black Enterprise, Essence — the founder story fits and these audiences over-index on brand loyalty
- "Atlanta startup building the operating system for a $15B industry" is a real story
- Press placements serve double duty: customer awareness and AEO entity building (AI systems cite press)
- Target at least one placed story at or before public launch

**Partnerships (Explore Post-Launch)**
- Car dealerships — new car buyers are primed detailing customers
- Apartment complexes and HOAs — captive audience, bulk booking potential
- Corporate fleet managers — longer term B2B play

## Operator Type Acquisition Strategy

FOAM serves two operator types. Acquisition messaging differs by type.

### Mobile Operators
- Primary pain: running business through DMs and Venmo, losing leads, no systems
- Primary message: "Stop running your business like a side hustle"
- Primary channels: Instagram/TikTok organic, blog content, cold outreach to active mobile detailers
- Search keywords: "mobile detailing app," "app for mobile detailers," "detailing business software"

### Fixed Location / Shop Operators
- Primary pain: empty bays, walk-in unpredictability, no scheduling system built for detailing
- Primary message: "Fill your bays. Keep your customers coming back."
- Primary channels: Google search (shop operators search more than they scroll social), foamauto.com/for-shops content, industry directory listings
- Search keywords: "auto detailing shop software," "detailing shop booking system," "detailing studio management app"
- Strategic note: this keyword universe has significantly less competition than mobile — first-mover content advantage is real and should be captured early

## National Expansion Acquisition Model

When FOAM opens Market 2, the acquisition playbook repeats with modifications:

**60 days before launch:** Publish city hub page on foamauto.com with "coming soon" messaging. Begin ranking for "[city] mobile detailing app" and "[city] detailing shops" before a single operator is onboarded.

**45 days before launch:** Content push targeting operators in that city. Same blog content pillar applied to the new market geography.

**30 days before launch:** Local citation building — Google Business Profile, Yelp, Apple Maps, local Chamber directory. NAP consistency established.

**Launch day:** Activate booking on city page. Operator profile pages go live and begin indexing.

**30-90 days post-launch:** Build neighborhood pages as operator density grows. Pursue local press. Referral loop activated in new market.

Market 2 priority candidates: Charlotte NC, Nashville TN, Miami FL, Dallas TX, Houston TX.

## What Not to Do at Launch
- Do not launch in multiple cities simultaneously
- Do not run customer acquisition ads before operator supply is ready
- Do not offer discounts so deep they attract the wrong customer or undervalue operators
- Do not add V2 features before V1 is working perfectly
- Do not pitch investors until the soft launch loop is proven
- Do not treat SEO as a later-stage task — the domain needs index history from day one
- Do not use stock photography in marketing — authenticity is core to the brand
- Do not publish blog content without proper on-page SEO structure (headings, schema, internal links)
