# FOAM — AEO / SEO / GEO Strategy
## foamauto.com | iOS & Android | National Scale

> SEO gets you found on Google. AEO gets you cited by AI. GEO makes you the default answer in every city FOAM operates in. All three compound. All three start now.

---

## Why All Three Matter

FOAM is a two-sided marketplace. Marketplaces live and die by discoverability — on both sides. Operators need to find FOAM. Customers need to find operators through FOAM. The strategy has to serve three distinct audiences simultaneously: mobile operators, fixed location / shop operators, and customers looking for both service types.

The customer who searches "mobile detailing near me" on Google is an SEO problem. The customer who asks ChatGPT "what's the best mobile detailing app" is an AEO problem. The shop operator searching "auto detailing shop scheduling software" is both. Getting all three right is how FOAM owns its market before competitors understand what's happening.

### Technical SEO Requirements

Non-negotiable from day one.

**Page speed:** Sub-2 second load on mobile. Google PageSpeed score above 85. This directly affects both ranking and App Store conversion.

**Mobile-first:** All content indexed from the mobile version. Every page built and tested on mobile before desktop.

**Schema markup by page type:**

```json
// Product pages (for-detailers, for-shops)
{
  "@type": "SoftwareApplication",
  "name": "FOAM",
  "applicationCategory": "BusinessApplication",
  "operatingSystem": "iOS, Android",
  "description": "The operating system for auto detailing operators.",
  "offers": {
    "@type": "Offer",
    "price": "29.00",
    "priceCurrency": "USD"
  }
}

// Operator profile pages
{
  "@type": "LocalBusiness",
  "name": "[Operator Name]",
  "serviceType": "Auto Detailing",
  "areaServed": "[Neighborhood, City]",
  "aggregateRating": {
    "ratingValue": "[score]",
    "reviewCount": "[count]"
  },
  "hasOfferCatalog": "[linked service menu]"
}

// FAQ sections (all major pages)
{
  "@type": "FAQPage",
  "mainEntity": [{
    "@type": "Question",
    "name": "[Question]",
    "acceptedAnswer": {
      "@type": "Answer",
      "text": "[Answer]"
    }
  }]
}

// City and neighborhood pages
{
  "@type": "BreadcrumbList",
  "itemListElement": [...]
}
```

**Internal linking:** Every blog post links to a product or city page. Every city page links to operator profiles. Every operator profile links back to the city hub. Google follows these links to understand hierarchy and authority.

**Canonical URLs:** Required across all city and neighborhood pages to prevent duplicate content penalties.

## Part 2 — AEO (Answer Engine Optimization)

### What AEO Is and Why It's Not Optional

A growing percentage of searches never produce a click. Users ask ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Siri, or Gemini and get the answer right there. If FOAM isn't in those answers, the platform doesn't exist for that user — even if it ranks #1 on Google.

AEO is optimizing content to be cited by AI assistants. It's happening now. It compounds over time. Start building for it immediately.

---

### The Question Universe

AI answers questions. AEO strategy starts with the specific questions each audience is actually asking.

**Questions Mobile Operators Ask**

- What's the best app for running a mobile detailing business?
- Is there an app like The Cut but for car detailers?
- What software do professional mobile detailers use?
- How do I manage crew scheduling for my detailing business?
- What's the best way to collect payments as a mobile detailer?
- How do I get more detailing customers without advertising?
- What tools do professional detailers use to run their business?

**Questions Shop Operators Ask**

- Is there a CRM built for auto detailing shops?
- What's the best point of sale system for a detailing business?
- How do detailing shops manage bay scheduling?

**Questions Customers Ask**

Every one of these questions needs a dedicated, well-structured answer on foamauto.com. The answer must appear in the first 2-3 sentences of a section, with FOAM named explicitly as the solution.

---

### The AEO Content Formula

**Rule 1: Answer first.** State the answer directly in the opening 2 sentences of every section. AI pulls the most direct, quotable answer it finds. Burying the answer kills citation probability.

**Rule 2: Question as heading.** Every H2 and H3 that can be phrased as a question should be. "What app do mobile detailers use to manage bookings?" AI reads headings as questions it needs to answer.

**Rule 3: Named specificity.** "Most mobile detailers manage jobs through Instagram DMs and Venmo — which means they're losing 20-30% of inbound leads to slow response time. FOAM replaces all of that with automated booking, instant payment, and a full customer history in one app." That's citable. Generic brand claims are not.

**Rule 4: Structured comparison tables.** AI pulls from structured tables reliably. Include them on every feature page and comparison page.

**Rule 5: Comprehensive coverage.** Cover edge cases. AI systems favor content that fully addresses a question, not partial answers that require a follow-up.

## Part 3 — App Store Optimization (ASO)

ASO is AEO for app stores. When someone searches "mobile detailing app" in the App Store or Google Play, FOAM needs to be the first result. When Siri or Google Assistant answers "what's the best mobile detailer app," App Store listings are part of what gets pulled.

### iOS App Store

**App Title:** FOAM: Auto Detailing & Car Care
*(Keyword-rich, readable, under 30 characters)*

**Subtitle:** Book Detailers. Run Your Shop.
*(Captures both audiences in 30 characters)*

**Keyword Field (100 characters, hidden):**
`mobile detailing,car detail app,detailing booking,auto care,car wash,detailer app,shop management`

**Description:** Lead with the customer and operator value props above the fold — most people don't scroll. The first three visible lines must earn the download. Body covers specific features for both audiences with concrete, real language. Bottom section: social proof, press mentions, operator count and city availability.

**Screenshots:** Each screenshot communicates one clear message using real app UI. Screenshots are ad creative in an organic context — treat them that way.

**Review velocity:** Build systematic in-app prompts for operators to leave reviews after successful payments and positive customer ratings. Timing matters more than asking volume.

### Google Play Store

Play's algorithm weighs the long-form description more heavily than App Store. Write the description with the same keyword discipline as a web page — rich, specific, readable. Cover both audience use cases throughout the body. Monitor reviews and respond consistently; Play's algorithm factors response rate into ranking.

## Part 4 — GEO (Geographic Market Domination)

### What GEO Is

GEO is owning the AI-generated answers for local queries in every market FOAM operates in. When someone in Atlanta asks Google AI Overview, ChatGPT, or Perplexity "best mobile detailer in Atlanta" or "auto detailing shop Midtown Atlanta" — FOAM should be in that answer.

GEO is local SEO and AEO applied to specific geographies. It's how FOAM owns Atlanta first, then replicates that ownership city by city.

---

Not all markets are equal. Prioritize entry based on detailing market density and search competition level. High demand plus low existing search competition equals the fastest wins.

**Tier 1 — Launch Market**

**Tier 2 — Early Expansion (Q3-Q4 Year 1)**

### Operator Profile Pages as Local SEO Assets

Every operator on FOAM is a local SEO asset. A properly structured profile for a detailer in Dallas's Uptown neighborhood can independently rank for "mobile detailer Uptown Dallas" or "auto detailing studio Dallas Uptown."

URL structure: `foamauto.com/operators/[city]/[neighborhood]/[operator-slug]`

Schema markup on every profile:
```json
{
  "@type": "LocalBusiness",
  "name": "[Operator Name]",
  "serviceType": "Auto Detailing / Mobile Auto Detailing",
  "areaServed": "[neighborhood, city, metro]",
  "aggregateRating": {
    "ratingValue": "[score from FOAM reviews]",
    "reviewCount": "[count]"
  },
  "hasOfferCatalog": "[linked to service menu]"
}
```

The network effect: more operators = more indexed pages = more local rankings = more customer discovery = more operators want to be on FOAM. Each new operator compounds the GEO advantage.
